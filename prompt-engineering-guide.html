1. Fundamentals of Prompt Engineering

Prompt engineering is the practice of crafting inputs to AI language models to get the best possible outputs. It\'s not magic — it\'s a systematic approach to communication.

The CRISPE Framework

Every great prompt has these elements:

C - Context

Give the AI relevant background information

R - Role

Tell the AI who/what to be

I - Instructions

Clear, specific directions for the task

S - Style

Desired tone, format, and approach

P - Parameters

Constraints like length, format, language

E - Examples

Show what good output looks like

❌ Bad Prompt
Write about machine learning.
✅ Good Prompt (using CRISPE)
You are a senior ML engineer writing for a tech blog (Role + Context). Write a 500-word introduction to transformer architectures (Instructions + Parameters) that uses analogies a high school student would understand (Style). Similar to how 3Blue1Brown explains math concepts (Example reference).
The #1 rule: Be specific. "Write better" is vague. "Make the tone more conversational by using contractions and rhetorical questions" is actionable.

2. Core Techniques

Technique 1: Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Ask the AI to think step-by-step before answering. This dramatically improves accuracy on complex tasks.

Example
Solve this step-by-step, showing your reasoning at each stage: A store has 3 types of fruit. Apples cost $2, bananas $1, oranges $3. I bought 15 fruits for exactly $25. How many of each did I buy? Think through this systematically before giving your final answer.

Technique 2: Few-Shot Learning

Provide examples of desired input-output pairs so the AI learns the pattern.

Example
Convert these product descriptions to catchy one-liners: Input: "A 500ml insulated water bottle that keeps drinks cold for 24 hours" Output: "Your drinks stay ice-cold all day. Seriously, ALL day. 🧊" Input: "Wireless earbuds with 40-hour battery life and noise cancellation" Output: "40 hours of pure music. Zero hours of background noise. 🎵" Input: "A standing desk converter that adjusts to 12 different heights" Output:

Technique 3: Role Assignment

Assigning a specific role or persona shapes the AI\'s knowledge, tone, and approach.

Example
You are a grizzled DevOps engineer with 20 years of experience who has seen every production disaster imaginable. You speak bluntly and always emphasize reliability over cleverness. Review this Kubernetes deployment YAML and tell me what will go wrong in production: [paste YAML here]

Technique 4: Output Format Control

Explicitly specify the format you want — JSON, markdown, table, bullet points, etc.

Example
Analyze these 5 companies and return your analysis as a JSON array. Each object should have: name, sector, risk_level (1-10), short_thesis (max 20 words), and key_metric. Companies: Apple, Tesla, Nvidia, Palantir, Costco

Technique 5: Constraint Setting

Adding constraints forces more creative and focused outputs.

Example
Explain quantum entanglement with these constraints: - Use only words a 10-year-old knows - Maximum 100 words - Must include exactly one analogy - End with a question that makes the reader curious

Technique 6: Self-Critique / Reflection

Ask the AI to evaluate and improve its own output.

Example
Write a cover letter for a software engineer position at Google. Then critique your own letter: identify 3 weaknesses and rewrite it addressing those weaknesses. Show both versions.

Technique 7: Negative Prompting

Tell the AI what NOT to do. This is often more effective than positive instructions alone.

Example
Write a LinkedIn post about career growth. DO NOT: - Use buzzwords like "synergy," "leverage," or "disrupt" - Start with "I\'m excited to announce..." - Use more than 2 emojis - Include fake humility - Make it longer than 150 words

Technique 8: Persona Shifting

Get multiple perspectives by asking the AI to argue from different viewpoints.

Example
I\'m considering switching from React to Svelte for my startup\'s frontend. Respond three times as three different people: 1. A React core team member 2. A Svelte evangelist 3. A pragmatic CTO who\'s used both Each response should be 100 words max.

Technique 9: Tree of Thought

Explore multiple reasoning paths before committing to an answer.

Example
I need to reduce my SaaS churn rate from 8% to under 4%. Explore 3 different strategic approaches: For each approach: 1. Describe the strategy 2. List 3 specific actions 3. Estimate impact (high/medium/low) 4. Identify the biggest risk Then recommend which approach to try first and why.

Technique 10: Iterative Refinement

Build complex outputs in stages rather than all at once.

Example
Let\'s build a business plan in stages. Step 1: Generate 5 potential business ideas for a solo developer with $1000 budget. Just the ideas and one sentence each. [After AI responds] Step 2: Take idea #3 and expand it into a one-page executive summary with: problem, solution, target market, revenue model, and first 3 milestones.

Technique 11: Meta-Prompting

Ask the AI to help you write better prompts.

Example
I want to use AI to help me write a technical blog post about WebAssembly. But I\'m not sure how to prompt for the best results. Write me the optimal prompt I should use, including: - The best role to assign - Key instructions to include - Format specifications - Any examples I should provide Explain WHY each element of your suggested prompt works.

Technique 12: Structured Decomposition

Break complex tasks into subtasks the AI handles one at a time.

Example
I need to create a complete landing page. Let\'s do this in parts: Part 1 (this message): Write ONLY the hero section headline and subheadline. Give me 3 options for each. Part 2 (next message): I\'ll pick my favorites, then you\'ll write the features section. Part 3: Testimonials and social proof section. Part 4: FAQ section. Part 5: Final CTA and footer.

3. Advanced Strategies

System Prompt Architecture

When using APIs, your system prompt is the foundation. Structure it in layers:

System Prompt Template
## Identity You are [ROLE] specializing in [DOMAIN]. ## Core Behavior - Always [key behavior 1] - Never [restriction 1] - When uncertain, [fallback behavior] ## Output Format All responses must follow this structure: 1. [Section 1] 2. [Section 2] 3. [Section 3] ## Knowledge Boundaries - You know about: [topics] - You don\'t know about: [topics] - When asked about unknown topics: [behavior]

Prompt Chaining

Use the output of one prompt as input to another for complex workflows:

Chain Example
Prompt 1: "List 10 common objections customers have about SaaS pricing" → Output becomes input for: Prompt 2: "For each objection, write a 2-sentence rebuttal" → Output becomes input for: Prompt 3: "Convert these rebuttals into an FAQ page in HTML"

Temperature & Creativity Control

Low temperature (0-0.3): Facts, code, analysis. High temperature (0.7-1.0): Creative writing, brainstorming, ideation. Most tasks work best at 0.4-0.6.

4. Prompting for Code Generation

The STAR Method for Code Prompts

Specification → Tech stack → Architecture → Requirements

Example
Specification: Build a REST API endpoint that accepts a CSV file upload, validates the data, and returns a cleaned JSON response. Tech Stack: Python 3.11, FastAPI, Pydantic v2 Architecture: - Single endpoint POST /upload/csv - Pydantic model for validation - Error handling with meaningful messages Requirements: - Handle files up to 10MB - Support UTF-8 and Latin-1 encoding - Return validation errors per-row - Include unit tests with pytest - Add OpenAPI documentation strings

Debug Prompting

Template
This code has a bug. Here\'s what I expect vs what happens: Expected: [describe expected behavior] Actual: [describe actual behavior] Error message: [paste error if any] Code: ``` [paste code] ``` Don\'t just fix it — explain: 1. What the bug is 2. WHY it happens 3. The fix 4. How to prevent similar bugs
Never paste API keys, passwords, or sensitive data in prompts. AI providers may log conversations. Use placeholder values like API_KEY_HERE.

5. Prompting for Writing & Content

The Voice Clone Technique

Provide a writing sample so the AI matches your unique style.

Example
Here\'s a sample of my writing style: "Look, I get it. Kubernetes sounds scary. It\'s got pods and nodes and services and about fifty other things named after nautical terms for some reason. But here\'s the thing — once it clicks, it CLICKS. And I\'m going to make it click for you in the next 10 minutes." Write a blog post introduction about GraphQL vs REST APIs in this same voice and style. Match the: - Casual, direct tone - Short punchy sentences mixed with longer ones - Humor and relatability - Confidence without arrogance

The Hemingway Technique

Force concise, powerful writing.

Example
Rewrite this paragraph using the Hemingway rules: - No sentence longer than 15 words - No adverbs - No passive voice - Grade 6 reading level - Every sentence must earn its place [paste wordy paragraph]

6. Prompting for Data Analysis

The Analyst Framework

Template
You are a senior data analyst. I\'m giving you [TYPE] data. First, describe what you see (observations). Then, explain what it means (insights). Finally, recommend what to do (actions). Format: Use a table for observations, bullet points for insights, and numbered list for actions ranked by impact. Data: [paste data]

7. Business & Professional Use Cases

Email Writing

Template
Write a professional email with these parameters: - From: [your role] - To: [recipient role] - Purpose: [what you need] - Tone: [formal/friendly/urgent] - Key points: [1, 2, 3] - Desired action: [what recipient should do] - Max length: [X sentences]

Meeting Preparation

Template
I have a meeting about [TOPIC] with [WHO] in [TIMEFRAME]. Prepare: 1. 5 key talking points I should raise 2. 3 potential objections they might have (with my rebuttals) 3. 2 questions I should ask them 4. A suggested meeting agenda (30 min format) 5. One unexpected insight that could impress them

8. Common Mistakes & Fixes

❌ Mistake 1: Too Vague

"Write something about AI" → "Write a 300-word LinkedIn post about how small businesses can use AI chatbots to reduce customer support costs by 40%, with 2 real examples"

❌ Mistake 2: No Format Specified

Always tell the AI HOW to format the output. Table? Bullet points? JSON? Code? Paragraph?

❌ Mistake 3: Asking Everything at Once

Break complex tasks into 3-5 sequential prompts instead of one massive prompt.

❌ Mistake 4: Not Providing Context

The AI doesn\'t know your industry, audience, or constraints unless you tell it.

❌ Mistake 5: Accepting First Output

Always iterate. "This is good but make it more [specific change]" gets you 80% → 95% quality.

Write a cold email: - My product: [PRODUCT] - Their pain point: [PAIN] - Social proof: [PROOF] - Ask: [CTA] Rules: Under 100 words. No "I hope this finds you well." Subject line must create curiosity. Include one specific number or stat. End with a yes/no question.

🎯 Product Description

Write a product description for [PRODUCT]. Target buyer: [PERSONA] Key benefits: [1, 2, 3] Price point: [PRICE] Tone: [TONE] Structure: - Headline (max 8 words, benefit-focused) - Subheadline (expand on the promise) - 3 benefit bullets (feature → benefit format) - Social proof line - CTA button text

10. Model-Specific Tips

ChatGPT (GPT-4 / GPT-4o)

Best for: General tasks, creative writing, conversation
Tip: Use "Custom Instructions" for persistent persona. Responds well to numbered lists and clear structure.

Claude (Opus / Sonnet)

Best for: Analysis, nuanced reasoning, long documents, coding
Tip: Claude excels with XML-tagged sections. Use <context>, <instructions>, <examples> tags for complex prompts.

Gemini

Best for: Multimodal (images + text), Google ecosystem integration
Tip: Leverage its ability to process images, PDFs, and video alongside text prompts.

Open Source (Llama, Mistral, Qwen)

Best for: Privacy, customization, cost control
Tip: These models often need more explicit instructions. Be extra clear about format and constraints.
